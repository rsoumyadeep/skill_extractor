DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Position Level: Associate Professor / Professor - Communication Design
Academic Area: Communication Design, Visual Communication, Graphic Design, Typography, Narratives and Storytelling, Publication Design, Branding and Identity, Motion and Animation, Information Design, New and Immersive Media, Design Studios, Research.
Description: Communication Design Faculty will teach and mentor undergraduate and postgraduate students across studio based and theory integrated courses. The role spans intermediate and advanced semesters, supporting skill development in visual communication, narratives, branding, information design, and new media. Key responsibilities include planning and delivering lectures, studios, and workshops aligned with course credit structures; guiding learners through iterative design processes and project development; contributing to assessments, evaluations, and academic reviews; and mentoring learners during studios, internships, and capstone projects; contribute to curriculum development and interdisciplinary collaboration in line with NEP frameworks.


Position Level: Associate Professor / Professor - User Experience Design
Academic Area: Digital User Experience Design, AR / VR / XR, User Experience Design, Interaction Design, Design Thinking, Usability Testing, World Building - Conceptual and Visual, and Human-Centered Design
Description: The position involves leading academic delivery, research, and industry engagement in UX design across undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Key responsibilities include planning and delivering lectures, studios, and workshops aligned with course credit structures; guiding learners through iterative design processes and project development; contributing to assessments, evaluations, and academic reviews; and mentoring learners during studios, internships, and capstone projects; contribute to curriculum development and interdisciplinary collaboration in line with NEP frameworks.


Position Level: Assistant Professor / Associate Professor
Academic Area: Foundation courses like Design Fundamentals, Drawing, Visualisation, Color Theory, Digital tools
Description: The role involves delivering design fundamentals, mentoring students, with a passion for nurturing creativity, innovation, and interdisciplinary learning across undergraduate programs. Key responsibilities include planning and delivering lectures, studios, and workshops aligned with course credit structures; guiding learners through iterative design processes and project development; contributing to assessments, evaluations, and academic reviews; and mentoring learners during studios, internships, and capstone projects; contribute to curriculum development and interdisciplinary collaboration in line with NEP frameworks.
