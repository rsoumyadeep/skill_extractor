DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
ORGANISATION & TEAM DESCRIPTION
Shell Lubricants Supply Chain (LSC) EMEA organisation covers more than 115 countries across Europe, Middle East and Africa. We provide ~1 Billion liters of lubricant per year to customers ranging from the world’s largest car manufacturers to small independent workshops, national utility companies to wind farms across the B2C and B2B customer groups.
The Supply Chain Optimisation & Development team, of which the Digital & Automation team is a part of, is accountable for all capital expenditure (~$80M per year) across EMEA. Within LSC, this team invests in the future of our assets, which consists of a network of 9 lubricants and grease manufacturing locations, to become modern, flexible, and adaptable to future customer needs.
The Automation Analyst is a member of the Digital & Automation team. The team is accountable for identifying, defining and implementing structural end to end digital and automation opportunities that create value for Shell. Driven by a dynamic landscape, automation, robotics, and data play pivotal roles for LSC EMEA.
The role reports into the Digital & Automation Manager. ROLE DESCRIPTION
Are you a data science enthusiast with a knack for supply chain optimisation, statistical analysis, and artificial intelligence? If so, keep reading!
As an Automation Analyst, you’ll be analysing and optimising our supply chain processes within LSC EMEA. You will be provided the opportunity to make a significant impact on our operations. This role involves working with data, modeling material flows, and optimization towards the most fit-for-purpose solutions. You’ll be using simulation software to identify the most optimal capital expenditure (CAPEX) investments, which will help to improve our supply chain competitiveness. For example, we’re significantly investing in automated mobile robots (AMR) across our sites. Typical questions the team addresses are;
· What are the best possible routes for the AMRs to navigate around our sites?
and
· How many AMRs do we need to avoid bottlenecks or risk congestion? And what should be the speed of our warehouses to match AMR capacity?
By applying your modeling expertise you will be supporting the team to get the right answers.
But there’s more! Over the years a lot of effort has gone into consistently collecting data-points across our supply chain, all integrated into one data platform. Though there’s more to do we see immense opportunities that are waiting to be explored. As an Automation Analyst you will be looking at opportunities to streamline and automate operations and processes. To do so you will be developing and deploying models in Python or Spark, depending on the size of the data. You’d be pivotal in bringing data closer to our decision-makers, enabling them to make informed decisions quickly. For example, by developing conversational AI interfaces that allow to query the data in natural language, making insights easily accessible and understandable for anyone.
All in all, as an Automation Analyst, you’ll be building and deploying your own models, as well as applying your modelling expertise to further advance our automation efforts across various parts of our supply chain!


YOU WILL BE EXPECTED TO
Analyse data:
· Conduct in-depth analyses of existing processes to identify potential areas for automation
· Run simulations to help design the ideal automation lay-out of our sites
· Model & simulate to identify the optimal CAPEX investments in LSC EMEA
· Recommend the best automation investment based on cost-benefit analysis
Develop and deploy models:
· Develop and deploy models using programming languages such as Python or Spark
· Deploy models into production environments and monitor their performance
Collaborate with cross-functional teams:
· Engage with various teams to identify automation opportunities
· Facilitate meetings with teams to discuss progress and address challenges
