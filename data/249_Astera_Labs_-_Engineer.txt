DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
As a new college graduate, you will be joining a team dedicated to developing state-of-the-art SoCs that enable next-generation connectivity solutions for future data centers. This role offers the opportunity to gain hands-on experience across multiple domains of chip design and verification, including:
 
**Design:**
Contribute to RTL development and perform RTL quality checks. Collaborate closely with Design Verification (DV), Design-for-Test (DFT), and Physical Design (PD) teams to ensure robust and efficient design implementation.
 
**Design Verification (DV):**
Develop and enhance design verification infrastructure, drive metric-based verification closure, and validate customer and system use cases. Work on various verification flows, including RTL, low-power, gate-level simulation (GLS), and emulation environments.
 
**Design-for-Test (DFT):**
Design and verify test structures to achieve target coverage and fault grading. Support silicon bring-up activities and assist in debugging and validating test vectors to ensure production readiness.
 
**Physical Design (PD):**
Engage in physical design activities such as floorplanning, placement, routing, clock tree synthesis (CTS), static timing analysis (STA), and physical verification. Perform power and reliability checks to ensure design quality and manufacturability.
 
This position provides an excellent opportunity to develop a strong foundation in SoC development, working alongside industry experts on cutting-edge connectivity technologies that power tomorrow’s data centers.


We are looking for passionate and motivated New College Graduates (NCGs) to join our VLSI Design team. Depending on your technical strengths, past academic work, and interests, you will be assigned to one of the following domains: RTL Design, Design Verification, Physical Design, or DFT (Design for Test).
