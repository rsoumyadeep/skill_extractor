DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
JOB Description - Process Equipment Designer
Process Equipment Design & Modular Manufacturing
Leumas · Bengaluru
Compensation - 15 LPA to 22 LPA
Leumas is seeking a Process Equipment Designer to drive the design and development of next generation process equipment and modular manufacturing systems.
This is a high-ownership engineering role responsible for translating process requirements into robust mechanical designs and executing across the complete build lifecycle—concept → design → prototype → fabrication → commissioning → iteration. The role requires strong mechanical fundamentals, disciplined design practices, and comfort operating in a fast-paced, build-first environment alongside process, controls, and software teams.


About Leumas
Leumas is a Bangalore-based deep-tech manufacturing company building a software-definedproduction platform for healthcare manufacturing. Its proprietary, full-stack Manufacturing Operating System orchestrates the entire manufacturing lifecycle - from formulation logic and material flow to production execution, quality control, and traceability - enabling modular, reconfigurable factories that scale with demand.
Leumas addresses structural challenges in global supply chains, rigidity, long lead times, and capital intensive, step function scale - through a modular, distributed manufacturing architecture that enables faster deployment, higher asset utilization, and materially improved business outcomes across product
life cycle.
Leumas supports two primary deployment models. For enterprise customers, it enables dedicated manufacturing suites that provide high levels of supply-chain control, compliance, and flexibility without the burden of traditional greenfield capex or non-resilient supply chains from traditional CDMOs. For healthcare innovators, the platform allows rapid configuration of production lines through software, enabling faster product launches, tighter quality control, and responsive scale-up or scale
down.
Leumas’ production systems currently support more than a dozen finished formulations and consumer health formats, including semi-solids (ointments, gels, lotions), oral solid dosage forms (tablets, capsules), and liquid formulations, with integrated QA and inspection capabilities.


The Role
As Process Equipment Designer, you will contribute to the mechanical design and delivery of process equipment platforms that enable modular, automated manufacturing.
You will work across system architecture, detailed design, prototyping, fabrication support, and commissioning stabilization. You will be expected to apply DFMA rigor, maintain high-quality documentation, and collaborate closely with cross-functional teams to deliver scalable, production
ready equipment.


What You Will Own
As Process Equipment Designer, you will be responsible for designing equipment that meets defined technical, operational, and manufacturability requirements while enabling rapid iteration and deployment.
1. Process Equipment Design & System Architecture
→ Translate process requirements into equipment concepts, architectures, and detailed mechanical designs.
→ Define interfaces, constraints, tolerances, and integration boundaries across modules.
→ Drive design decisions that balance performance, reliability, serviceability, and manufacturability.
2. End-to-End Build Cycle Execution
→ Participate across the full lifecycle: concept → design → prototype → fabrication → commissioning → iteration.
→ Support fabrication and assembly with engineering clarity, build-time issue resolution, and rapid design iterations.
→ Contribute to commissioning stabilization and continuous improvement loops.
3. Design for Manufacturing & Assembly (DFMA)
→ Apply DFMA principles to improve manufacturability, reduce complexity, and increase repeatability.
→ Standardize components, sub-assemblies, and fabrication approaches where appropriate.
→ Contribute to value engineering initiatives for cost, throughput, and maintainability improvements.
4. CAD, Drawings & Documentation Control
→ Create and maintain CAD models, drawings, and BOMs using SolidWorks / Fusion 360 (or equivalent).
→ Maintain structured documentation practices including revision control and design traceability.
→ Support design reviews through clear engineering rationale and documentation readiness.
5. Cross-Functional Integration
→ Work closely with Process, Controls, Software, Quality, and Production functions to deliver integrated systems.
→ Participate in structured reviews and resolve multidisciplinary integration issues during build and commissioning.
→ Ensure mechanical designs are aligned to operational execution and long-term scalability needs.
