DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Introduction
LTM is a global technology consulting and digital solutions company that enables enterprises across industries
to reimagine business models, accelerate innovation, and maximize growth by harnessing digital technologies.
As a digital transformation partner to more than 700 clients, LTM brings extensive domain and technology
expertise to help drive superior competitive differentiation, customer experiences, and business outcomes in a
converging world. Powered by 87,000+ talented and entrepreneurial professionals across more than 40+
countries, LTM— a Larsen & Toubro Group company — solves the most complex business challenges and
delivers transformation at scale. For more information, please visit LTIMindtree is now LTM | It’s time to Outcreate
Role Details
Business Unit: Global Technology Office Proposed Role: Research Engineer (Neuromorphic)
Education: M.E/M.TECH
Stream: Neuromorphic Computing, Electrical
Engineering, Signal Processing, Dynamical Systems
backgrounds
Experience Required: 0 Location: Bengaluru
Responsibilities & Skill Requirements:
- Neural model design: Design SNN or other brain-inspired architectures and Reservoir Computing models for
multi-dimensional temporal data.
- Neural Coding: Develop efficient encoding schemes (Rate, Temporal, or Phase coding) to translate use case
specific metrics into spike-based representations.
- Software Implementation: Utilize neural frameworks to map algorithms onto neuromorphic hardware
backends like Intel Loihi, IBM TrueNorth, Innatera Pulsar.
- Deployment and testing: Deploy models on neuromorphic simulators, hardware (e.g. Intel Loihi 2, SpiNNaker)
as well as conventional hardware. Optimize models for energy-efficiency and real-time performance.
- Benchmarking: Conduct comparative studies between neuromorphic models and conventional Deep
Learning (RNNs, LSTMs) regarding accuracy, latency and power. You will need to document approaches,
manage experiments & do detail result analysis. Iteratively refine algorithms for compactness and efficiency
- Application Building: Build ITOps / Cybersecurity / Biomedical application prototypes for real-time anomaly
detection, log pattern recognition, low power temporal pattern recognition, sensing and signal processing at
the edge, and predictive system scaling.
- Collaboration: Collaborate with academic partners to incorporate latest research development, work with
SMEs to understand real-world scenarios & constraints.
- Knowledge creation: Contribute to publications or patents as appropriate.

Required Skills & Competencies
Programming: Strong Python (NumPy, SciPy, PyTorch). Familiarity with C/C++ would be a good additional skill
for low-level Process Models.
Mathematical Foundations: Linear Algebra & Calculus, Differential Equations, Dynamical Systems modeling &
Stochastic Processes (Optional).
Neural Models & Algorithm Development: Deep understanding of neuronal Spiking Neural Network (SNN)
models like the Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF), Reservoir Computing etc.
Learning Rules: Exposure to STDP, E-Prop, Surrogate Gradient Descent for SNN training.
Data Science: Knowledge of signal processing and time-series analysis (FFT, wavelets, windowing). Working
knowledge of Machine Learning (Scikit-Learn) & Deep Neural Networks (RNNs, CNNs) using Pytorch.
Frameworks: Proficiency in framework(s) to map algorithms onto hardware backends. Exposure to braininspired computing frameworks/SDKs/libraries like Lava (Intel), snnTorch, Nengo, ReservoirPy, simulators like
Norse, Brain2/NEST is preferable. (The names are indicative – other similar frameworks / platforms is fine)
- Neuromorphic Experience (Optional plus): Hands-on experience with neuromorphic hardware platforms or
FPGA/ASIC design tools. Knowledge of Intel’s NxSDK or similar toolchains is a bonus. Exposure to eventdriven sensors (e.g. DVS camera) or asynchronous computing is beneficial.
Neuromorphic systems expertise(Optional plus): Understanding of neuromorphic architectures and their
applications in various fields.
Overview: As a founding member of the Neuromorphic Computing track, you will be working on application of
brain-inspired algorithms in IT / Security Operations and other applications requiring low energy, real-time,
edge intelligence.
You will design, implement, and benchmark algorithms involving Spiking Neural models, neural coding
schemes, STDP and other bio-inspired approaches that leverage the asynchronous, event-driven nature of
neuromorphic hardware to solve complex problems.
The role involves doing applied research, and you will be instrumental in transforming state-of-the-art
neuromorphic computing research into impactful prototypes for real-world problems. This role involves
working in an interdisciplinary environment. You will be required to
adapt to new learning rules and architectures as research progresses.
Education: Candidates with Masters / PhD in Computer Science, Neuromorphic Engineering, Computational
Neuroscience is well aligned for this position.
Candidates with Electrical Engineering, Signal Processing, Dynamical Systems backgrounds may also apply if
they have deep interest in this field.
Project experience in the area of neuromorphic computing is a plus.
