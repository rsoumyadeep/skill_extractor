DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Job Description for Hardware roles:
Physical Design/STA "This role, will provide opportunity to work on Physical design, using Synthesis -Automatic Placement and Route flow, from RTL to GDS, including sign off for Timing, Reliability and Layout verification, using Industry standard tools for complex APR partitions with high frequency targets.
Candidate will be working on wide variety of activities related to Synthesis, Floor planning, Placement, Clock Tree Synthesis, Routing, Timing convergence, Scan, Hard Macro integration, and Physical convergence. They will work on constraint development, flow optimization to meet design requirements, by working with RTL developers, CAD team and EDA vendors"
Silicon Design
Contribute to the development and documentation of tools, flows, and methodologies utilized for silicon design. Assists in analyzing design efficiency and quality gaps to improve automation and scalability across projects. May contribute to internal tool development and crossteam enablement. As an intern, learns and applies knowledge, builds skills, and explores future career opportunities through handson experience and projects that support Intel business goals in a collaborative environment.
Digital/Analog Design
Contributes to the design, simulation, and verification of digital and/or analog circuits. Works to ensure robust circuit implementation and integration and may also assist in schematic entry, timing analysis, and optimization for power, performance, and area. As an intern, learns and applies knowledge, builds skills, and explores future career opportunities through handson experience and projects that support Intel business goals in a collaborative environment.
Hardware Design
Supports the design and development of platform hardware and board development including evaluation and selection of board components and PCB layout. Supports board bringup, debug activities, and cross functional collaboration to meet electrical, thermal, and mechanical requirements. Applies design principles to optimize performance, manufacturability, and cost. As an intern, learns and applies knowledge, builds skills, and explores future career opportunities through handson experience and projects that support Intel business goals in a collaborative environment.
CPU Design
CPU Design Projects for leading Intel Client Projects. The scope includes Physical Design, Logic Design, Pre-Silicon Verification
Support the design, implementation, and verification of cutting‑edge processor architectures. The role involves writing and debugging RTL in System Verilog, collaborating on microarchitecture features such as pipelines, caches, and execution units, developing testbenches using industry‑standard verification methodologies, and analyzing performance or functional issues through simulation tools. The ideal candidate has strong fundamentals/interest in computer architecture and digital design, hands-on experience with HDLs, and a passion for building high‑performance, energy‑efficient compute systems.
