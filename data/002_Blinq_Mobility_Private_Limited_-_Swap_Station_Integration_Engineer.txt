DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Company Overview
Blinq Mobility is a fast-growing startup revolutionising the urban commute by addressing two
fundamental challenges in electric mobility:
● Reducing charging downtime through proprietary quick battery-swapping technology
● Building purpose-built, lightweight electric vehicles using advanced plastic–composite
material architectures
Our vehicles and infrastructure are designed ground-up for high-utilisation urban operations,
with a strong emphasis on engineering-led product development.
Why Join Blinq Mobility?
At Blinq, you will work on production-critical hardware systems that directly enable
next-generation electric mobility. We offer:
● Ownership of real, deployed automation systems
● Exposure to vehicle, infrastructure, and system-level engineering
● Mentorship from senior engineers with deep industry and motorsport backgrounds
● A startup culture focused on hands-on execution, rapid iteration, and engineering-first
decision-making
Job Role
As a Swap Station Integration Engineer, you will be responsible for designing and
developing the rail-guided automation systems used in Blinq Mobility’s battery-swapping
platform. You will work on precise mechanical motion, actuation, sensing, and system
integration to enable fast, safe, and reliable battery swaps.

---

ADDITIONAL DETAILS
Key Responsibilities
● Design and develop the rail-guided motion system for Blinq Mobility’s
battery-swapping platform, ensuring precise and repeatable battery movement from
the vehicle latching mechanism to the charging bay
● Automate the end-to-end battery-swapping process, including alignment, transfer,
latching, and secure placement
● Integrate mechanical systems with actuators, sensors, and control logic for smooth
and reliable operation
● Collaborate closely with mechanical, electrical, and software teams to optimise
system performance and system-level integration
● Conduct testing, validation, and troubleshooting of rail-guided and automation
systems under varied operational and environmental conditions
● Drive continuous improvement initiatives focused on swap speed, positional
accuracy, robustness, and operational reliability
Preferred Student Background
This role is best suited for students with hands-on experience in:
● Robotics and automation projects
● Mechatronic system design involving actuators, sensors, and motion control
● Rail-guided or linear motion systems
● Hardware-focused engineering teams or projects
