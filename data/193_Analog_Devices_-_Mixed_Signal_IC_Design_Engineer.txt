DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Analog Devices is seeking a Mixed Signal IC Design Engineer to join the Hearable and Wearable Business
Unit to participate in analysis, research, design, simulation, characterization, and release-to-production
of high-performance, low power, integrated circuits.
The successful candidate will join a team with a track record of producing breakthrough innovative
architectures for Class-D amplifiers and delta-sigma DAC’s and ADC’s, to name a few. The successful
candidate must have a strong, intuitive capability in solving complex challenges. The candidate will be
self-motivated and be able to work effectively within a team.
Responsibilities
• Architecture investigation, design and verification of analog and mixed-signal blocks
• Collaboration with digital design and design verification teams for block and system integration
and verification at chip level
• Interface with product definition, product, applications, and test, engineering to successfully
bring new products from initial concept through production release
• Evaluate and troubleshoot complex integrated circuit problems in the laboratory
Education and Experience Requirements:
• Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering in CMOS IC design
• Understanding of a wide range of circuits such as high-gain amplifiers, switched-capacitor
circuits, comparators, integrators, precision voltage references, LDOs
Preferred Qualifications
• Experience in ADC/DAC design
• Experience in audio IC design with a passion for audio
• Experience in Cadence schematic capture and SPICE simulation tools
• Experience in delta-sigma modulator design
• Experience with MATLAB or other modeling tools, and specifically their application to delta
sigma modulator design
• Understanding of digital signal processing concepts
