DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Job Description:
We are seeking a highly motivated and talented Research Scholar to join our Power Electronics research group. The candidate will work on cutting-edge research projects related to power conversion systems, control techniques, energy efficiency, and advanced power semiconductor devices. This is an excellent opportunity to contribute to innovative research in the field of power electronics and develop expertise in experimental setups, simulation tools, and practical design applications.
________________________________________
Key Responsibilities:
• Conduct independent research in power electronics, focusing on areas such as:
o DC-DC, AC-DC, and DC-AC converters
o Wide-bandgap devices (SiC, GaN)
o Advanced modulation and control strategies (e.g., PLL, predictive control)
o Power quality analysis and mitigation
o Grid-connected inverters and renewable integration
• Design, simulate, and develop power electronics prototypes using tools such as MATLAB/Simulink, PLECS, PSIM, or LTspice.
• Perform experimental validation of research concepts using laboratory setups (e.g., hardware-in-the-loop testing, FPGA-based control implementation).
• Prepare technical reports, project documentation, and thesis work in a structured manner.
• Collaborate with academic and industrial research partners.
• Stay up-to-date with the latest developments in power electronics and related technologies.
________________________________________
Required Qualifications:
• Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Power Electronics, or a related field.
• Strong understanding of power electronics principles, semiconductor devices, and control strategies.
• Proficiency in simulation tools such as MATLAB/Simulink, PLECS, PSIM, or equivalent.
• Hands-on experience in power electronics hardware development and testing is preferred.
• Good analytical and problem-solving skills.
• Strong communication skills for documentation and publication.
________________________________________
Desired Skills (Optional but Preferred):
• Experience with real-time control platforms (e.g., Plecs RT Box, dSPACE, OPAL-RT, TI C2000, STM32).
• Knowledge of grid codes, power quality standards, and EMI/EMC concepts.
• Familiarity with hardware design (PCB layout, gate driver circuits, power modules).
• Experience with wide-bandgap devices (SiC, GaN) applications.
