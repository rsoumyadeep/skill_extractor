DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Team and role overview
The Responsible Investing (RI) team sits within the Impact Group and plays a crucial role in reinforcing BII’s reputation as a responsible investor, ensuring it meets its regulatory commitments and enhancing private sector standards on financial crime risk management, corporate governance and environmental and social standards. The team has ownership of shaping and operationalising BII’s Policy for Responsible Investment (PRI), ensuring it is fit for purpose in BII’s investment activities.


The team comprising professionals based in the UK, India, Kenya, Nigeria and South Africa is involved in all BII investments and plays a critical role in ensuring that BII’s PRI is consistently applied across all transactions. As well as managing the assessment of investments in pipeline, the team is also responsible for portfolio management of investees, working on improved performance of responsible investing standards, capacity building, issues management and market shaping initiatives that support improved standards more widely and help build value across BII’s portfolio


An analyst in BII’s RI Sector team will have a direct reporting line to a Manager within RI.


Purpose
The RI Sector teams is structured into three sector teams aligning to our investment pillars: Financial Services (FSG), Infrastructure & Climate (I&C), and Industries, Technology & Services (ITS). The RI Sector teams support a wide range of transactions from the earliest stage of origination through pipeline development, due diligence, approval and portfolio monitoring.


An Analyst in an RI sector team will work on the Responsible Investing workstream for both pipeline and portfolio stage transactions. This will include supporting the operationalising of the PRI requirements and fostering a positive team culture.


An Analyst will have the ability to develop a consistent track record for risk management on the responsible investing disciplines of environmental and social (E&S), and with a desire to build experience across disciplines and develop as many transferable skills over time as possible.


Key Responsibilities
Transactional Work
• Support and contribute to completion of relevant E&S risk assessment and due diligence, at early-stages of transaction appraisal.
• Support RI team in conducting site visits and implementation of PRI with investees E&S personnel at investees assets
• Shadow and support on transactions in pipeline and portfolio
• Develop sound judgement and procedural adherence in escalating material risks or incidents, either to your line manager, sector vertical head or other internal functions, as relevant.
 Process Integration & Portfolio Oversight
• Support to ensure consistent application of BII’s E&S requirements and policies across BII’s investees.
• Timely escalation of material RI risks/concerns identified in the course of portfolio monitoring to subject matter experts, Directors or Team Heads as appropriate, working with these colleagues to analyse and remediate.
• Support with data extraction and data-based analytics and insights as required for portfolio management, risk trend analysis and internal and external RI reporting.


Stakeholder Engagement
• Support the development and roll-out of strategically important initiatives, ensuring commercially relevant and precedent-setting positions are well articulated.


Training & Knowledge Sharing
• Shadow the delivery of RI training for internal teams and external investees.
• Support the development of briefing materials, case studies and other materials that showcase how E&S business practices contribute to commercial and impact success.


Develop broader cross disciplinary RI understanding
• Develop cross disciplinary RI competencies via learning by doing, formal and informal internal and external training and proactive engagement with others in RI team.
• Foster a strong team culture and act as a culture carrier for RI team.


Regional Element
• Local expertise: develop knowledge of local regulations, trends, listing requirements, industry associations, sustainability/compliance headwinds and market intelligence and regularly share with the RI team.
• Strategy and pipeline: shadow and support the integration of RI inputs into regional strategies and investment pipeline. This could include identifying potential risks/concerns related to the implementation of BII’s Policy on Responsible Investing and Fossil Fuel Policy and making timely escalations to Directors or Team Heads to ensure efficient origination activities. On occasion, meeting with prospective investees to introduce BII’s RI requirements and value creation approach.
• Relationship management: actively manage relationships with local internal stakeholders and investment team members.

About BII
British International Investment (BII) is the UK’s leading development finance institution, wholly owned by the UK Government. Our primary mission is to address global development challenges through strategic investments that promote sustainable and inclusive economic growth.


BII focuses on catalysing economic development, creating jobs, and building resilient economies in some of the world's most complex and underserved markets. We invest in businesses and projects that prioritise inclusive development, environmental sustainability, and innovation.


Our approach is characterised by rigorous investment standards, high governance principles, and a commitment to delivering measurable development impact. Our team consists of highly skilled and passionate professionals dedicated to making a tangible difference globally.


We foster a collaborative and intellectually stimulating work environment that values curiosity, innovation, and professional growth. Our culture balances high performance with a strong emphasis on wellbeing, ensuring our employees can achieve their full potential while contributing to our mission.
