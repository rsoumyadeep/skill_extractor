DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
About Bajaj Auto Ltd- 
With more than 18 million motorcycles sold in over 70 countries, the Bajaj brand is truly ‘The World’s Favourite Indian’. It is India’s No.1 motorcycle exporter with two out of three bikes sold internationally carrying a Bajaj badge. Bajaj Auto is the first two-wheeler and three-wheeler company in the world to have reached a market capitalisation of INR one trillion and continues to be the world’s most valuable two and three-wheeler company. There are different departments where we hire graduate trainees- one such department is Operations under which this role is for Engineering vertical.


Engineering department-
In Bajaj Auto, Engineering department is the core function which ensures optimistic process flow in the components manufacturing. Hence plays a very important and key role in Manufacturing its products. Engineering Machine & Automation Technology department is the driving core behind the company's manufacturing capability, responsible for the design, development, deployment, and maintenance of cutting-edge production systems. It operates at the intersection of mechanical engineering, robotics, software development, and digital technology, driving efficiency, quality, and the realization of Industry 4.0 principles.


Key Responsibilities-

➢ Automation & Robotics: Assisting in the design and application of Special Purpose Machines (SPMs) and robotic cells (Collaborative & Autonomous Mobile Robots).
➢ Digital Integration: Supporting the deployment of IIoT, Digital Twin, and MES (Manufacturing Execution Systems) for real-time data analysis and system optimization.
➢ Machine System Fundamentals: Learning the practical application of hydraulic, pneumatic, lubrication, and cooling systems in manufacturing equipment.
➢ Control & Software: Supporting the development and troubleshooting of Electrical Design and Control Systems for automated equipment.
➢ Assembly & Commissioning: Participating directly in the assembly, tryout, and quality control checks for new manufacturing assets.
