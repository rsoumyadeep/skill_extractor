DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Renesas is one of the top global semiconductor companies in the world. We strive to develop a safer, healthier, greener, and smarter world, and our goal is to make every endpoint intelligent by offering product solutions in the automotive, industrial, infrastructure and IoT markets. Our robust product portfolio includes world leading MCUs, SoCs, Analog and power products, plus Winning Combination solutions that curate these complementary products. W e are a key supplier to the world’s leading manufacturers of electronics you rely on every day; you may not see our products, but they are all around you.
Renesas employs roughly 21,000 people in more than 30 countries worldwide. As a global team, our employees actively embody the Renesas Culture, our guiding principles based on five key elements: Transparent, Agile, Global, Innovative, and Entrepreneurial. Renesas believes in, and has a commitment to, diversity and inclusion, with initiatives and a leadership team dedicated to its resources and values. At Renesas, we want to build a sustainable future where technology helps make our lives easier. Join us and build your future by being part of what’s next in electronics and the world. 
We are looking for a passionate and self-driven Digital Design Engineer to join our hardware design team. This is an excellent opportunity for a New College Graduate (NCG) to grow and contribute to the development of cutting-edge digital systems, including [ASICs/SoCs/FPGAs]. You will work under the guidance of senior engineers and be involved in all aspects of digital design and verification.
Key Responsibilities:
Understand and interpret system-level specifications and convert them into digital design blocks
Develop RTL designs using Verilog, SystemVerilog, or VHDL
Perform simulation and functional verification of RTL modules
Assist in synthesis, timing analysis, and FPGA implementation (as applicable)
Collaborate with cross-functional teams (architecture, verification, and physical design)
Document design architecture, specifications, and validation plans
Basic Qualifications:
Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Electronics or Electrical Engineering
Solid understanding of digital logic design principles
Familiarity with RTL coding (Verilog or VHDL)
Exposure to simulation tools (e.g., ModelSim)
Strong analytical and debugging skills
Good written and verbal communication
Preferred Skills (Nice to Have, but Not Mandatory):
Knowledge of SystemVerilog and UVM
Familiarity with ASIC/FPGA design flows
Understanding of bus protocols like AXI, AHB, or APB
Experience with scripting (Python, TCL, Shell)
Hands-on project or internship involving FPGA/ASIC design
Exposure to power management concepts (e.g., voltage regulation, DC-DC converters)
Awareness of SDC constraints and STA basics.
