DETAILED JOB DESCRIPTION
Job Title: Distributed Ledger & Blockchain Engineer
Position Overview
The world is moving beyond Web2, and decentralized systems now power the next era of trust and transparency. As a Distributed Ledger & Blockchain Engineer, you will design and build smart contracts, prototypes, and scalable consensus systems that redefine secure information exchange at a global scale.
Key Responsibilities
• Design and prototype distributed ledger architectures for secure data exchange and transaction traceability.
• Develop smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps) using Ethereum, Hyperledger, or Solana ecosystems.
• Implement cryptographic primitives such as zero-knowledge proofs, Merkle trees, and hash-based consensus mechanisms.
• Build interoperability bridges and APIs between blockchain networks and enterprise systems.
• Create scripts and monitoring tools for node performance, consensus stability, and chain analytics.
• Research and test scalable consensus protocols (PoS, BFT, DAGs) for performance and resilience.
• Collaborate with cybersecurity teams to audit contract logic, prevent exploits, and ensure transaction integrity.
• Experiment with tokenization, verifiable credentials, identity solutions, and supply chain traceability models.
Required Qualifications & Skills
• Strong understanding of computer science fundamentals, distributed systems, or cryptography.
• Proficiency in programming languages such as Solidity, Go, Rust, TypeScript, or Python.
• Familiarity with blockchain frameworks (Ethereum, Hyperledger Fabric, Polygon, Solana, Avalanche).
• Knowledge of consensus tools (Tendermint, Raft, HotStuff) and smart contract development tools (Truffle, Hardhat, Remix).
• Ability to work with data and analytics tools like IPFS, The Graph, Prometheus, and Grafana.
• Understanding of security and cryptographic concepts (zk-SNARKs, elliptic curve cryptography).
Preferred Attributes
• Innovative mindset with a passion for decentralized technologies.
• Strong problem-solving skills and willingness to experiment.
• Ability to work independently and collaboratively in a research-driven environment.
• Interest in open-source contributions and emerging blockchain trends.
Commitment to building secure, transparent, and fair digital ecosystems.
